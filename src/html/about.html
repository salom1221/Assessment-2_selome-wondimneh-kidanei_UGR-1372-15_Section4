About Freya Clothing

Freya Clothing was founded with a vision to celebrate individuality, self-expression, and the beauty of the unconventional.


Inspired by Freya, the Norse goddess of love, beauty, and war, the brand embodies a duality that reflects strength and elegance. We chose the name "Freya" because it symbolizes empowerment, creativity, and a fierce commitment to standing out. At Freya Clothing, we blend modern trends with timeless aesthetics to create pieces that resonate deeply with those who dare to be different.

Freya Clothing Aesthetics

Emo (Gothic):

Our emo-inspired designs channel raw emotion and introspection, fusing oversized silhouettes, dark tones, and edgy details. Think band tees, ripped layers, and dramatic accents that reflect a deep connection to music and self-expression.

Dark Academia:

For the intellectual and mysterious, our dark academia collection features structured tailoring, muted tones, and vintage-inspired pieces. Plaid patterns, blazers, and flowing skirts create a look that bridges sophistication and rebellion.

Y2K:

Nostalgia meets modernity in our Y2K aesthetic. Metallic fabrics, playful crop tops, flared pants, and bold accessories celebrate the tech-fueled optimism and rebellious spirit of the early 2000s.

Grunge:

Our grunge line is rooted in authenticity and rebellion. Flannel shirts, distressed denim, combat boots, and layered textures bring a sense of effortless cool that captures the heart of 90s counterculture.

Our Designers

  • Luna Blackthorn

    • Luna is a visionary designer inspired by the mysticism of the moon and the haunting beauty of gothic art. Her designs often merge delicate lacework with bold, edgy cuts, creating pieces that exude mystery and elegance.


  • Damien Ashcroft

    • Damien brings a raw, rebellious energy to Freya Clothing. Drawing influence from 90s grunge and Y2K streetwear, his designs are a perfect mix of distressed textures, bold patterns, and oversized silhouettes.


  • Ivy Ravenshade

    • Ivy is the embodiment of dark academia, blending classic tailoring with modern minimalism. Her designs are inspired by old libraries, Gothic architecture, and the timeless appeal of vintage fashion.
